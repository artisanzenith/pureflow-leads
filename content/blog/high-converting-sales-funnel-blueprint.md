A funnel isn't a diagram on a whiteboard—it's the actual path prospects take. When conversion stalls, one stage usually bleeds more than the rest.

## Top of funnel: Message-market fit

Traffic without resonance is expensive noise. Test headlines against one ICP before scaling spend.

## Middle of funnel: Education and proof

Case studies, demos, and comparison pages address objections before sales calls. Prospects who self-educate close faster.

## Bottom of funnel: Friction audit

Every extra form field, unclear pricing element, or slow page load costs deals. Run quarterly funnel walkthroughs with fresh eyes.

## Post-purchase expansion

Onboarding emails and success milestones drive expansion revenue. The funnel doesn't end at checkout.

## Instrument everything

Connect ads, pages, CRM, and billing so you see full-journey conversion—not channel silos.
